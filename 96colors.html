96 Colors

Where does one start when creating a three-dimensional letterform? What is the purpose of a three-dimensional letterform if the user can not use it? It just becomes a paperweight. This was my initial inspiration, why not create three-dimensional letter form you could use, but who would want to? I cut up crayons into smaller pieces and let them set in a mold.

"They look like something space would throw up, can I color with them?"

proclaimed my younger cousin of seven. I knew who my audience was, children. Only they could see the unused potential of a writing tool. As he colored I created the rest of the alphabet, seeing how he was using the letter form I noticed that it changed the way the letter form looked.

The Exhibit

I was exhibited at a gala opening for three-dimensional letterforms. I used coloring books as the basis for the interaction. A small table was set down for people to get low to the ground, so they too could color like the children they once were.

The Display Font

I was was asked to what extent could these letterforms become a display font and still keep their childlike qualities. This is the outcome.
