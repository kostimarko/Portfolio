Default Design

A conversation between two people is never the way the transcript makes it seem. One person usually does not talk and wait for the other person to say something, there is a playfulness to the conversation.

A conversation has pauses, one person tries to talk over the other causing interruption, sometimes it gets to be a rant about something thing one could lose interest in.

I took a lot of liberty here in the way this book was constructed. The transcript comes from a conversation between Rob Giampietro and Rudy VanderLans. While there was no audio recording of the conversation for me to make my basis on where the conversation had any interruptions.

I, as the user of the article, interject my own interruptions, I put the pauses or the interruptions as I felt fit. A large question is then asked. Is what the author had written and the way the reader interrupts the essay accepted in the same voice?

Have we been reading our favorite novels with the emphasis in the wrong places? The difference between emphasis in conversations is easily detectable, in print however, this emphasis becomes harder to draw attention to.
